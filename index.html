Team Seas

Beaches

Marine debris like plastics, paper, wood, metal, and other materials are found on beaches worldwide. Aquatic animals and seabirds confuse plastic for food. Trash left on beaches will be carried away by sea to oceans and other parts of the world.

Oceans

Once junk touches any part of a body of water it becomes mobile. It moves by winds and water currents. Debris carried this way ends up in oceanic gyres where currents are weak. Plastic in gyres will slowly break down into smaller plastic and will slowly accumulate toxic chemicals making the environment surrounding the gyres hazardous to aquatic life.

Food Chain

Filter feeders consume microplastic and are devoured by large fish. The larger and fattier the fish, the more it is likely to see microplastic in their guts. Contaminated fish are bought and sold on fish markets. Most fish we eat are exposed to toxic chemicals either by eating plastic or just by inhabiting near oceanic gyres. We do not know the full effect of aquatic animals consuming plastic and us consuming contaminated fish.

Mission

With your help #TeamSeas will work with local authorities, Ocean Conservancy, and its partners to remove millions of pounds of plastic from beaches, rivers, and oceans worldwide. We'll also send professional crews equipped with the best available science and technology to clean up the most vulnerable oceans affected.
